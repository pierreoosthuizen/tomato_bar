## Description

> Updated only with new app versions. 4000 character limit.

A simple, focused Pomodoro timer that lives in your macOS menu bar.

Tiny Tomato Bar helps you stay productive with timed work intervals and scheduled breaks — the core of the Pomodoro Technique. Start a session with one click, and the timer counts down discreetly in your menu bar. When time is up, you get a notification and a sound alert. Then take your break and go again.

FEATURES

• Configurable intervals — Set work, short rest, and long rest durations from 1 to 60 minutes
• Long break cycling — After a configurable number of work intervals, automatically switch to a longer break
• Auto-start and auto-stop — Schedule the timer to begin and end at specific times on specific days of the week
• System sounds — Choose from 14 built-in macOS sounds for start and end alerts, or disable them entirely
• Menu bar countdown — See your remaining time at a glance in the menu bar
• Global keyboard shortcut — Start or stop the timer from any app
• Actionable notifications — Skip a break directly from the notification
• Launch at login — Start the timer automatically when you log in
• URL scheme — Control the timer from scripts and shortcuts using veridian:// URLs
• Event logging — JSON log of all state transitions for personal analytics

DESIGN

Tiny Tomato Bar stays out of your way. No dock icon, no floating windows — just a small tomato in your menu bar. Click it to adjust settings, or let it run on its own with auto-scheduling.

Fully sandboxed with no special permissions required.

LOCALIZATION

Available in English, Korean, and Simplified Chinese.

OPEN SOURCE

Tiny Tomato Bar is free and open source, based on TomatoBar by Ilya Voronin. Source code available on GitHub.
